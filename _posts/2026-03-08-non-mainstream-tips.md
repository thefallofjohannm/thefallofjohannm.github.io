## Transportation

First of all, download [IDOS](https://idos.cz/vlakyautobusymhdvse/spojeni/). IDOS is the perfect tool for finding connections 
within a city. It is possible to start just with __your location__ and  __address__ as your final destination. It will search for the nearest stations
(bus, tram, train, boat...) and then the quickest route. You can check the length and duration of the route, and you can also
display the map to see, what you will pass through and where the stations are. Furthermore, IDOS allows you to buy tickets in the 
app. Finally, this app is especially useful if you travel within the whole country as it can search for connections from one 
city to another city, from a city to a village; or even from one country to another country (e.g. a trip to Berlin, Graz, Gdansk, 
Budapest, Bratislava etc.). It mostly works in other countries too, but mainly for international trains and buses, probably 
not that well for local public transport.

I highly recommend downloading [Mapy.cz](https://mapy.cz). While it is just another app for maps, it shows all the stations
very clearly and it is impossible to get lost or not to find where a bus, tram, or boat departures from. The numbers of houses
or buildings are displayed too, which I believe it is fairly pratical. It is also possible 
to download a region map for an off-line use. The biggest highlight of this app is the hiking routes which are excellently 
managed in Mapy.cz. The app works really well outside of Czechia too, so I recommend keeping it after this trip.

For Prague only, an app [PID lítačka](https://pidlitacka.cz/cs) is generally recommended. I am personally not the biggest fan,
however I should mention it as it facilites on-line ticket purchase. Otherwise the ticket machines are in every metro station, 
and currently in most of the trams and buses. Ticket inspections are common, so do not try to ride without a ticket.

When buying a ticket for public transport, I personally recommend buying a 3-day ticket or 1-month ticket (coupon) as it 
generally comes cheaper than buying several 30-minute tickets. This applies especially for students who can prove that 
they study (usually ISIC card).

IDOS, Mapy.cz or PID lítačka are not necessary, you can use Apple Maps or Google Maps if it works better for you, but 
I highly recommend getting at least IDOS as it is super easy to use, allows you more freedom when choosing the transport
and also makes planning much easier as you can __swipe right__ to see when the next tram is coming (e.g. you need to take
metro --> metro --> tram, and you know you will not take the tram immediately, so you just swipe the tram in the app to see,
that it goes in next 5 minutes, 10 minutes, 15 minutes etc.).

Finally, if you're looking for something more outside of Prague (I might amplify my  collection one day), 
search in [visitczechia.com](visitczechia.com). There you will find seasonal events, hiking destinations and many more.

#### Food
(Potrefená husa, Kozlovna, U fleků)
- Vepřo knedlo zelo - Pork, cabbage, dumplings (baked)
- Guláš s knedlíky - this is the peak, beef with sauce and dumplings (in restaurants they usually serve more kinds), it will never dissappoint you
- Svíčková na smetaně - the most famous czech food; baked and liquified root vegetables with beefs and dumplings with whipped cream on the top
- Plněné knedlíky - anything that is like… stuffed dumpling with fruit, it is sweet
- Uzené knedlíky - this is with meat and most likely cabbage
- Buchtičky se šodó/krémem - you might not find it in prague restaurants, its sweet, little fluffy "bread" with a cream
- Chlebíčky - this is very universal food (look up google, there are many kinds, it is not standarlized) it’s almost like tapas, its a piece of bread with somehting, you can get it in random places such as Mňam Mňam, or Libeřské Lahůdky, theaters but surprisingly not in café or pubs. I recommend for example Antonínovo pekařství, look which kind they have and get 2 or 3 pieces of something that looks good; can be a snack, always non-sweet
- Kuřecí/vepřový řízek - chicken/pork schnitzel - it is just as common in Czechia, usually with potatoes, or potatoes salad 
- Smažený sýr - literally Fried Cheese - whilst it doesnt sound special, until recently it was only in Czechia. (But nowadays they makes little fried cheeses in McDonald, KFC etc... ) but thats not the same experience. Smažený sýr s hranolkami (french fries) with a Tatarská omáčka (common) or Mayo (I prefer), somehow this dish is a typical Czech thing and you don't find it in other countries
- Grilovaný hermelín - pretty much the same, it is grilled brie (not exactly brie but this kind of cheese) - probably not in restaurants, maybe in pubs
- Nakládaný hermelín - this is in pubs but it is not as lit as the aforementioned 
- Pochoutkový salát - salad - you'll find it in supermarkets in the fridges, it's a little box, about 150 grams, it has written Vlašský, Pařížský, Ďábelský or Rybí Salát on it. It's basically... depends but the basics are ham, onion, mayo and for example peas. It might not sound good but it's great with Rohlík, and when you are on the "student budget" this makes an excellent snack. I'm not claiming this is typical Czech, but I didnt see it in Maribor
- Pomazánka - this means "spreading" - this is also very general thing, it can be made from "anything". If you are in Antoninovo pekařství, get "Vajíčková pomazánka" (egg) and buy some bread, put it on the bread and enjoy. In supermarkets they sell many of these, the "typical ones" are probably vajíčková (egg), česneková (garlic), nivová (blue cheese), sýrová (cheese); you can also get tuňáková (tuna), rybičková (fish) and many others. In supermarkets, there is a corner of fresh food, get for example 100 g of Česneková pomazánka, 100 g of Vajíčková pomazánka and spread it on little pieces of bread --> you just made Jednohubka. 
- Bramborový salát - potatoe salad - could be in restaurants with schnitzel or again packed in supermarkets
- Tatarák - I dont think it is czech, but see for yourself - it is raw beef with topinky (fried Czech bread). Get it in restaurants as an appetizer (or the main dish)
- Vepřový jazyk - Pork tongue - probably not exactly typical but at least rare to see, in Prague you can get this in Dlouhá (restaurant)
- Bramboráky - more less Potatoe pancakes - it is fried usually served with some smoked meat. 
- Moravský vrabec - moravian sparrow - but it doesn't contain a sparrow - very similar to Vepřo knedlo zelo, but it has sauce
- Pečená kachna se zelím - baked duck with cabbage (and dumplings) - you won't get the whole duck, only a part (my grandma makes this when the whole family gathers) 
- Sekaná - meatloaf, probably not in restaurants 
- Česnečka - garlic soup - if you can get a soup, this one is traditional
- Kulajda - the same - creamy dill, mushroom, and potato soup with poached egg, is another proud representative of the sweet and sour dish family - not my personal favorite, I dont like dill nor mushroom
- Zelňačka - this is a cabbage soup, a little spicy - we got it at the slovenian dinner
- Šišky s mákem - I doubt you'd find it anywhere - potato cones with poppy seeds
- Žemlovka - this is a dessert, prepared from rohlik, bread or houska with apple and sugar, it is baked
- Utopenci - "drowned man" - 99 % chance to be found in pubs, sausages that are sliced open, filled with mustard, onion, chilli, allspice, and bayleaves and then thrown overboard into a large jar with sweet and sour brine and left to their own devices
